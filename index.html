Short Bio

Dr. Hengyun (Neil) Li is a tenured Associate Professor in the School of Hotel and Tourism Management (SHTM) at the Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU). Since joining the faculty in August 2018, Dr. Li has brought a wealth of knowledge and expertise, having previously earned his Ph.D. and served as an instructor at the University of South Carolina, USA. His research delves into business intelligence and artificial intelligence, the platform economy, and multimodal big data analysis and prediction for the hospitality and tourism industry.

Dr. Li has developed extensive collaborations with international researchers and has made significant contributions to leading journals. His work has garnered over 3,400 citations, reflecting an h-index of 35, underscoring his significant impact in the field. His research has been prominently featured in leading journals and conferences in tourism/hospitality management, business administration and AI. He is one of the World’s Top 2% Most-Cited Scientists by Stanford University (2024). Dr. Li is also the winner or finalist of four prestigious international conferences.

As a principal investigator, Dr. Li has secured nearly 10 million Hong Kong dollars in research funding from esteemed organizations, including the National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC-Young Scientist Found & General Program) twice, the Hong Kong Research Grant Council (RGC-GRF & ECS & DAG) on three occasions, the Hong Kong University Grants Committee (UGC), and the Hong Kong Scholars Program. His expertise is further recognized through his service on the editorial boards of seven top-tier academic journals, such as Tourism Management (ABS 4), Annals of Tourism Research (ABS 4), and the Journal of Travel Research (ABS 4).

Dr. Li's contributions have earned him accolades such as the “PolyU Faculty Outstanding Young Researcher” award from the Hong Kong Polytechnic University and the “USC Breakthrough Graduate Scholar” recognition from the University of South Carolina. He has also been invited to share his research on business intelligence, hospitality/tourism big data and artificial intelligence at renowned universities and organizations worldwide, including Virginia Tech University, Pennsylvania State University, University of South Carolina, and University of Hawaii in the US; Zhejiang University, Xi’an Jiaotong University, Nankai University, and Southeast University in China; the Silk Road International University in Uzbekistan; and the Group NAO from Europe. His dedication to advancing the interdisciplinary fields of AI, business, and hospitality and tourism management continues to inspire and influence both academia and industry.
